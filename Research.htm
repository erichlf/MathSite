Current Research

My research interests are centered around the development of mathematically justified models and corresponding efficient, accurate algorithms for grand challenge problems at the frontier of computational science and engineering. My current interest is in the application of these ideas to climate modeling, such as the Quasi-Geostrophic Flow Equations (QGE), flow in porous media with an emphasis on density dependent flow and contaminate transport. In both areas I emphasize the use of finite elements (FE), including error analysis and efficient algorithms such as the two-level method.


Climate Modeling:
Figure 1: Subpolar and Subtropical Gyres of the Mid-Atlantic The Quasi-Geostrophic equations (QGE) are simplified models of large scale oceanic or atmospheric flows and are often used in climate modeling \cite{Dijkstra05}. The QGE describe a double gyre system that are characteristic of mid-latitude oceans \cite{Dijkstra05, Pedlosky, Vallis06} (See Figure 1). For the QGE, we consider the pure streamfunction form
\begin{equation} -\Delta \psi_t + Re^{-1} \Delta^2 \psi + J(\psi, \Delta \psi) - Ro^{-1} \psi_x = Ro^{-1} F, \label{eqn:QGE} \end{equation} where \(\psi\), \(Re\), and \(Ro\) are the streamfunction, the Reynolds number, and Rossby number, respectively, while \[ J(\psi,\Delta \psi) = \psi_x\, \Delta \psi_y - \psi_y\, \Delta \psi_x \] is the Jacobian and \(F\) is the external forcing. To completely specify the system we consider the boundary conditions \(\psi = \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial \mathbf{n}} = 0\). In \cite{Foster} we applied a finite element (FE) discretization to the QGE. This is the first time a \(C^1\) FE has been used to discretize the QGE. In \cite{Foster} we presented rigorous error analysis and numerical experiments.

Most FE discretizations of the QGE are for the streamfunction-voriticity formulation of the QGE, and the reason is simple. The pure streamfunction form is a fourth order PDE, requiring \(C^1\) FEs for conforming FEs, such as the Argyris element. However, the advantage of the pure streamfunction formulation is the reduction of two variables to a single variable. In addition to algorithm development, I have proven in \cite{Foster}, for the first time, optimal error estimates for the FE discretizations of the QGE.

Theorem Let \(\psi\) be the solution to the stationary QGE and \(\psi^h\) be the solution of the FE formulation of stationary QGE. Assume that the small data condition is satisfied: \begin{equation} Re^{-2} \, Ro \geq \Gamma_1 \, \| F \|_{-2} . \label{eqn:small_data_condition_dual} \end{equation} Furthermore, assume that \(\psi\in H^6(\Omega) \cap H^2_0(\Omega)\). Then there exist positive constants \(C_0, \, C_1 \text{ and } C_2\) that can depend on \(Re\), \(Ro\), \(\Gamma_1\), \(F\), but not on the mesh size \(h\), such that \begin{align} |\psi - \psi^h|_2 &\le C_2 \, h^4 , \label{eqn:H2Error} \\ |\psi - \psi^h|_1 &\le C_1 \, h^5 , \label{eqn:H1Error} \\ \|\psi - \psi^h\|_0 &\le C_0 \, h^6 . \label{eqn:L2Error} \end{align}


Flow in Porous Media:
Figure 2: Depiction of Seawater Intrusion
As populations increase the demand for groundwater increases. In fact, groundwater amounts to one-third of total freshwater consumption in the world. Seawater intrusion is a major limiting factor on the utilization of groundwater in coastal regions. As the seawater wedge progresses landward existing pumping wells become saline and therefore have to be abandoned \cite{Bear99}.

Harold R. Henry \cite{Henry60,Henry64} considered the problem of seawater intrusion into coastal aquifers for the case of an isotropic homogeneous confined aquifer, in which there is a steady flow of freshwater toward a stationary body of higher density seawater. A constant flux of freshwater is applied to the inland boundary, while a body of higher density seawater is on the seaward side (See Figure 3). Seawater therefore, intrudes from the seaward boundary towards the inland boundary until an equilibrium is reached between the heavier seawater and the lighter freshwater recharge (See Figure 2).

Figure 3: New solver solution to Henry's Problem showing isochlor concentration \cite{Foster06}


Henry's semi-analytic solution to seawater intrusion has been used as a benchmark for numerical simulations of seawater intrusion for many years \cite{Simpson03}. However, its ability to use parameters that are more representative of narrow zones of diffusion has been limited \cite{Abarca07}. The method used by Henry to evaluate his analytic solution has been tempered by the limited ability to increase the number of Fourier coefficients used, and this may possibly lead to the inability of those methods to solve Henry's problem using parameters that would simulate narrow zones of diffusion.

In \cite{Foster06}, I developed a new solver for Henry's semi-analytic solution to seawater intrusion. The new solution method improved Henry's semi-analytic solution as benchmark for numerical models of realistic seawater-freshwater interfaces, by allowing for the use of more realistic model parameters and thus improves the worthiness of Henry's problem as a benchmark for numerical models.
